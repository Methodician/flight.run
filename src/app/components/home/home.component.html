What We Do

We work with entrepreneurs and idea people to bring their ideas to fruition.

Entrepreneurs need their startup idea to be viable, not just minimal. And it's the idea and the business that need to be viable, not just the product. We define a viable state as meeting one or more of the following three criteria:

Meteoric Takeoff

Rapid mass-market adoption of a new product or service.

Usually best suited for low-margin, mass-market ideas. Your ultimate goal is to have lots of customers and deep market penetration. In this case it can be a good idea to lose money early on. Give something away to attract early adopters. A rapid rise in early adoption could be your ticket. This could create a monetization opportunity, or just look really good to investors there by making you more presentation ready.

Cash Flow Positive

More money is generated by the product than it consumes.

It can be surprisingly easy to get here in the right context. This approach is usually best suited for sales-heavy, high-margin products or services (usually B2B) where one big customer could net you thousands of dollars per month. At the outset this allows you to reinvest and grow but it also looks really good to investors, making your product or service more presentation ready.

Presentation Ready

The product or idea is compelling investors and early adopters.

You can get here without the other two, but realistically most investors these days won't give you the time of day unless you have something to show and some skin in the game. I don't think every software startup should be focused on that big investment or aquihire but if they are, they'll need more than a good idea, some fancy slides, and a sparkling smile to get it.
